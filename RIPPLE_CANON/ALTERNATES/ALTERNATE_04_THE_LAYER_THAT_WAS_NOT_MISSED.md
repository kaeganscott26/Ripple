## 📖 Alternate Story

The ticket prints wrong.

TABLE 34.

Burger.

No onion.

No pickle.

Cheddar.

Fries.

Sauce side.

No sauce named.

Leo sees the blank and feels the rush behind it. He asks once. Priya says she will check. The fries are dying. The rail is filling. The grill cook says ranch because everybody likes ranch in this state.

So ranch goes out.

The plate leaves.

The mistake becomes ceramic.

Six minutes later, Priya returns too fast.

Thirty-four is upset.

Honey mustard.

Whole remake.

Contaminated.

In the canon path, the screen above expo cuts through the rush and names the missing layer.

Not here.

Here, the screen stays normal.

Tickets.

Times.

Red numbers.

No white text.

No ghost app.

No query asking what else entered through the plate.

Just the visible problem.

A customer is being difficult about sauce.

That sentence is efficient. It travels quickly. It gives the kitchen something to hold without stopping the rush. It protects Leo from intimacy, Priya from collapse, the grill cook from grief, and Darren from the kind of managerial softness that has no button in the POS system.

[[Leo]] remakes the burger with honey mustard and anger sharp enough to keep his hands moving.

[[Mara]] enters this branch as witness denied. There is testimony in the room, but no one wants to hear what the plate is testifying to.

[[Jamal]] enters as output form. The incident becomes a customer complaint, a comp, a remake, maybe a note in a shift log. The form has no field for grief.

[[Maren]] enters as mood pressure. The kitchen feels justified because justification is easier to work inside than uncertainty.

[[Dev]] enters as access closed. Table 34 has a door to memory, but the staff only sees the door as inconvenience.

[[Teodor / Scott]] reads the branch as the danger of a system that can move orders faster than it can notice people.

Darren comps the meal with the restaurant smile.

He does not learn about the husband because he does not ask the second question.

He says:

> Sorry about that. We remade it correctly.

Correctly.

That word lands badly.

The woman at Table 34 looks at the correct burger, the correct fries, the correct honey mustard, and understands that the room has repaired everything except the thing that broke.

She eats two fries because eating nothing would make people stare.

She leaves cash because she does not want to owe anyone more explanation.

Priya reads the tip as judgment.

Leo reads the untouched burger as entitlement.

The grill cook reads the remake as disrespect.

Darren reads the comp as resolved.

The empty chair reads nothing.

Nobody has asked it to.

By the end of the rush, Table 34 is reset. New guests sit there laughing over appetizers. The chair across from the woman is occupied by someone else before the surface has fully dried.

The room uses the table again.

That is the horror of the missed layer.

Not that nothing happened.

That something happened and the room successfully returned to function without understanding it.
