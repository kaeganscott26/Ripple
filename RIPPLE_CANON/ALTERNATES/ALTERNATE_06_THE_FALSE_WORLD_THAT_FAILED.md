## 🧭 Core Mirror

[[Chapter 06]] shows the danger of a false world: a story comfortable enough to replace reality.

This alternate asks what happens when the false world fails before it can become the room.

The opposite ripple is **reality returning early**.

---

## 📖 Alternate Story

The false world arrives with good language.

It sounds helpful.

It offers a version of the room where no one has to feel the hard thing directly. It smooths the edge, gives the event a cleaner name, and lets everyone pretend the conflict has already been understood.

But this time the room checks the comfort.

[[Mara]] hears the command hidden inside the care. She asks who benefits if everyone accepts the easier version.

[[Jamal]] sees the form trying to become official before reality has finished speaking.

[[Maren]] notices the mood improve too quickly and distrusts relief that requires silence.

[[Dev]] sees the door that the false world wants to paint on the wall and asks whether it opens.

[[Teodor / Scott]] recognizes the danger of building a framework that people cannot disagree with safely.

The false world cannot survive being inspected by all five pieces.

---

## 🟢 Intervention Point

Someone asks:

> Does this story make reality clearer, or does it make reality easier to avoid?

The room does not reject comfort.

It rejects comfort that replaces truth.

---

## 🔴 Missed Intervention Point

The false world becomes the room's preferred explanation.

If missed, people begin calling the cage a safe place because the cage has softer language than the outside.

---

## 🌊 Ripple Event

The room adopts a new test:

> Any helpful story must remain safe to question.
