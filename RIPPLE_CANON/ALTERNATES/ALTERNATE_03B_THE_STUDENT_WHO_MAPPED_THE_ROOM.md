## 📖 Alternate Story

Jamal stops at the choke point with the hall pass in his hand.

Marcus stands where the path narrows.

Devin laughs.

Eli watches.

The cart waits beside the trash cans like it has no idea it is part of anything.

Jamal's body does the math.

If I lower my eyes, I lose.

If I speak, it escalates.

If I touch him first, I am suspended.

If I do nothing, I carry it all day.

The trophy case reflects the hallway back at him.

ROOM DETECTED

ARCHITECTURE ACTIVE

The words do not save him.

They make something visible enough to use.

Instead of shoving Marcus, Jamal raises the laminated hall pass and presses it flat against the trophy case glass. His other hand pulls out his phone. He does not record Marcus's face. He records the gap.

Cart.

Trash cans.

Marcus's shoulder.

Bathroom door.

Camera angle.

His own route blocked without anything that looks like blocking.

Marcus says, “Bro, you recording?”

Jamal says, “I'm mapping.”

That sentence is so strange that the performance stumbles.

[[Jamal]] does not escape the hallway. He changes the kind of evidence the hallway has to become.

[[Mara]] enters as witness logic. She knows the difference between recording a person to punish them and recording a room to make the room count.

[[Maren]] enters as safety pressure. Mapping slows the body down enough to keep heat from becoming contact.

[[Dev]] enters as access design. Once the path is visible, the question is no longer whether Jamal should walk away. The question is where walking is possible.

[[Teodor / Scott]] reads the branch as the birth of board logic: space becomes data, data becomes move, move becomes consequence.

A classroom door opens.

Ms. Aranda steps out.

This time, she does not arrive after the shove.

She arrives at the map.

She sees Jamal holding the pass against the glass. She sees Marcus in the gap. She sees the cart, the bins, the camera, the bathroom door, the exact absurd geometry of a hallway that asked a child to regulate architecture with his nervous system.

She says one sentence:

> Nobody move.

The hallway freezes.

Not because it is safe.

Because someone with authority finally noticed its shape.

In the office, Principal Voss begins with the expected script, but the script cannot settle. Jamal has photos. Ms. Aranda has the map. The hallway has entered the room before the form can close.

There is still consequence.

Jamal is assigned a restorative conference because he escalated verbally and refused to follow the original hallway command.

Marcus is assigned a conference too.

Devin and Eli are not allowed to disappear into audience status.

But nobody is suspended.

The bigger consequence belongs to the building.

Move the cart.

Move the bins.

Open the east bathroom.

Add transition supervision.

Change the camera angle.

Create a student room-map form.

By the end of the day, the west stairwell has become the first playable board space.

Not fixed.

Named.

Mapped.

Playable.
