## 🧭 Core Mirror

[[Chapter 01]] says a hidden room became visible because a camera entered it, the broadcast moved through American living rooms, and one family eventually moved toward Teodor.

This alternate asks what happens if the camera enters the room, but the signal never reaches the house that would have become his future.

The opposite ripple is **signal without receipt**.

The world still changes.

But not his world.

Not yet.

---

## 📖 Alternate Story

The program airs.

The camera walks through the rooms.

The children are seen.

The correspondent speaks. The footage moves across satellites, networks, televisions, cables, antennas, eyes, kitchens, and couches.

But in the house that would have changed Teodor's life, the television is off.

Maybe someone is working late.

Maybe the channel is changed before the segment begins.

Maybe the sound is too low.

Maybe the phone rings.

Maybe the room is busy with its own life and misses the door opening in Romania.

That is all it takes.

Not evil.

Not destiny.

A missed signal.

The camera becomes a hand for someone else, but not for him.

In this branch, Teodor is not immediately carried into the American room. But unlike Alternate 01A, the door does not close forever. The broadcast still changes enough of the world that adoption systems shift, agencies grow, churches talk, newspapers follow, and paperwork routes begin to multiply.

The ripple reaches Teodor late.

Late is not the same as never.

Late has its own wound.

When Teodor is older, a different family considers him. They have more information than the first wave had, more warnings, more language, more caution, more fear, and less innocence. They know adoption is not a clean rescue story. They know attachment may be complicated. They know love will not erase the first room.

This makes them more prepared.

It also makes them more afraid.

[[Mara]] enters as the delayed witness. She carries the record that should have arrived sooner.

[[Jamal]] enters as the updated form: more ethical, more documented, more careful, and therefore slower.

[[Maren]] enters as the mood of caution. The room wants to help but fears harming what it touches.

[[Dev]] enters as delayed access. The door is real, but it takes longer to open because everyone has finally learned that doors have consequences.

[[Teodor / Scott]] enters the new room old enough to remember leaving.

That changes everything.

He keeps fragments of language.

He remembers hallways, faces, cold floors, the smell of soup, an adult voice, the feeling of waiting in a line, the shape of a window. None of it is clean enough to testify. All of it is strong enough to return later.

When he crosses the ocean, he does not cross as an infant carried entirely by adults.

He crosses as a child who knows something is being removed.

The Oklahoma room still becomes possible.

The father layer still becomes possible.

The piano still might arrive.

[[AIFRED]] still might exist.

[[Ripple]] still might exist.

But the theory changes.

This Teodor does not build Ripple to locate a wound before language.

He builds Ripple to understand the violence of translation.

He remembers enough of the first world to know he lost it.

He learns enough of the second world to know he survived it.

The game becomes less about invisible origin and more about competing origins.

Two languages.

Two homes.

Two names.

Two rooms that both claim him and neither fully explains him.

## 🟢 Intervention Points

### 🟢 Intervention Point 01 — The Late Door Tells the Truth

Before adoption, someone tells Teodor plainly:

> You are leaving one real life and entering another. The first one does not stop being real because the second one begins.

If this lands, the crossing becomes grief plus movement instead of movement disguised as rescue.

### 🟢 Intervention Point 02 — The New Family Does Not Pretend

The receiving family refuses the clean story.

They do not say:

> You are lucky now, so the old room is over.

They say:

> Love is arriving late. We will not call late love the same thing as no loss.

If this lands, the father layer becomes complicated but stable.

## 🔴 Missed Intervention Points

### 🔴 Missed Intervention Point 01 — The First Room Is Erased

The new room tries to make belonging easier by acting like Romania is only backstory.

If missed, Teodor learns that being loved requires losing evidence.

### 🔴 Missed Intervention Point 02 — Prepared Fear Becomes Distance

The adults know too much about possible damage and become careful in a way that feels cold.

If missed, preparation becomes another wall.

## 🧠 Game Consequence Map

### What Changed From Canon

Canon Teodor is adopted early enough that the first room becomes body-state more than narrative memory.

Alternate 01B Teodor is adopted later. He remembers fragments of the first room and experiences adoption as conscious translation.

### Future Made Impossible

- Ripple cannot be built as a theory of pure pre-language origin.
- The father layer cannot be simple rescue-after-loss.
- The first room cannot remain mostly unreachable.
- The game cannot treat origin as invisible only; it must treat origin as partially remembered and contested.

### Future Made Possible

- Teodor can still become the builder.
- [[AIFRED]] can still exist.
- [[Ripple]] can still exist.
- The game gains Translation Mechanics.
- [[Dev]] gains stronger access logic earlier.
- [[DREAM_LAYER]] becomes stronger because fragments have images.

### Ripple Carrier

Primary carrier: [[Teodor / Scott]]

Secondary carrier: [[Dev]]

Witness stabilizer: [[Mara]]

Form stabilizer: [[Jamal]]

Safety stabilizer: [[Maren]]

### Character Effects

- [[Teodor / Scott]] keeps Builder Path, but Builder Path becomes Translation Path.
- [[Dev]] gains threshold authority.
- [[Mara]] protects the first room from erasure.
- [[Jamal]] slows the form so the new room cannot overwrite the old one.
- [[Maren]] tracks whether preparation becomes care or distance.

### Artifact Effects

- [[Father Layer]] remains active but gains Late Arrival modifier.
- [[Split Layer]] becomes mandatory.
- [[Dream Layer]] becomes more concrete.
- [[Signal Released, No Receipt Required]] becomes root artifact.
- [[Boundary Handrail]] prevents the receiving room from calling erasure healing.

### Later Chapter Impact

- [[Chapter 02]] Trigger becomes translation pressure.

- [[Chapter 08]] 0826 becomes two-calendar pressure: birth date, adoption date, remembered date, chosen date.

## 🎮 Move Logic Notes

If the run is on Alternate 01B, Teodor can still create Ripple, but the game must alter the builder path.

Builder Path becomes Translation Path.

When a move references origin, the board must check:

- Was the origin erased?
- Was the origin remembered?
- Did the receiving room make room for both?
- Did the artifact protect translation or force replacement?

If [[Dev]] is active, access spaces gain extra weight.

If [[Mara]] is active, witness spaces can preserve first-room memory.

If [[Jamal]] is active, forms can either document both origins or overwrite one.

If [[Maren]] is active, safety checks whether preparation feels like warmth or distance.

Primary rule:

> If the broadcast arrives late, the game still exists, but it becomes a translation engine.

## 🎮 Game Meaning

This alternate teaches the board that late rescue is still movement, but it is not the same ripple as early rescue.

When a signal arrives late, the story does not vanish.

It translates.
