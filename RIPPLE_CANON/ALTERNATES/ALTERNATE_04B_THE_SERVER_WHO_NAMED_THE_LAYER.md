## 📖 Alternate Story

The ticket prints wrong.

TABLE 34.

Burger.

No onion.

No pickle.

Cheddar.

Fries.

Sauce side.

Priya knows the sauce is missing the second she sees Leo's face.

She says she will check.

Then the dining room catches her by the sleeve.

Table 12 needs ketchup.

Table 7 says their wings are cold.

A child spills lemonade.

Darren asks why Table 19 has no silverware.

By the time she reaches Table 34, the plate is already there.

Ranch beside the burger.

Wrong.

The woman is sitting very still.

That is what Priya notices first.

Not angry still.

Not customer-service still.

Funeral still.

The woman looks at the ranch cup the way people look at phones that will never ring again.

Priya's first instinct is to defend herself before anyone accuses her.

Third double.

No sleep.

New job.

Rent due.

Too many tables.

Too many buttons.

Too many ways to be wrong in front of strangers.

But the woman says:

> This was supposed to be honey mustard.

And Priya hears something under it.

Not because she is wise.

Because exhaustion sometimes thins the wall between people.

She says the sentence no server is supposed to say during a rush:

> Is this about someone?

The woman looks up.

The dining room continues moving. Forks scrape plates. A blender screams at the bar. The host laughs too loudly near the front door. A birthday song starts in the far corner with dead-eyed clapping.

But Table 34 becomes separate from the restaurant.

A room inside the room.

The woman's face changes before her voice does.

> My husband ordered it this way.

Priya does not know what to do with her hands.

So she folds them in front of her apron like someone at a service.

[[Priya]] is not one of the main pieces yet, but this branch makes her a temporary witness token.

[[Mara]] enters through her: witness does not have to be expert to be real.

[[Jamal]] enters as form refusal. The ticket is wrong, but the record is not allowed to close around wrong sauce only.

[[Maren]] enters as the room's tenderness before it becomes policy.

[[Dev]] enters as the small access door between dining room and kitchen.

[[Teodor / Scott]] reads the branch as proof that a playable move can begin with the smallest correct question.

Priya walks back to the kitchen differently.

Not fast.

Not slow.

Careful.

Leo sees her face and gets ready for the complaint script.

Instead she says:

> Her husband died. The burger is his. The honey mustard is part of it.

The kitchen does not become kind all at once.

The rail is still full.

The grill cook still hates remakes.

Leo still feels the expo pressure pulling at every rib.

But the story enters the kitchen before judgment hardens.

The remake is not just a remake anymore.

It is a memory being re-plated.

Leo says:

> Fire it clean.

The grill cook says nothing for once.

Darren comps it before anyone asks.

Priya brings the new plate herself.

She does not over-apologize.

She does not make the woman's grief perform for the table.

She says:

> I am sorry we moved the memory wrong.

The woman covers her mouth.

Priya thinks she has said too much.

Then the woman reaches for her hand.

The contact lasts two seconds.

Maybe three.

The kitchen never sees it.

That matters.

Some interventions do not need the whole room to witness them to be real.

Later, Leo asks what happened, and Priya tells him. He listens. This time Leo is not the first person to name the missed layer. He becomes the person who helps preserve it.

At the end of the night, Priya writes the note from memory and clips it to the office board:

> Sometimes the sauce is the memory.

Darren almost takes it down because it is not company-approved language.

He leaves it.

That is his intervention.

Not dramatic.

Just not erasing the sentence.
