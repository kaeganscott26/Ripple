## 🧭 Core Mirror

[[Chapter 02]] says Mara creates the gap herself: she delays the response, buys crackers, deletes the message, and then calls her sister.

This alternate asks what happens if the witness arrives before Mara has to choose alone.

The opposite ripple is **witness before command**.

The trigger still enters the room.

The craving still speaks.

But another person steps into the gap before the old path can become private.

## 📖 Alternate Story

Mara knows the sound before she knows what it means.

Ding.

Ding.

Ding.

The gas station is quiet in the wrong way. The pumps wait outside. The fluorescent lights make every object look like evidence. The phone rests beside the register with one letter in the text box.

D.

One letter.

One door.

The customer in the red jacket has already left, but the laugh remains. The body does not understand that resemblance is not return. Her stomach drops. Her shoulders tighten. The old car appears behind her eyes. The cracked dashboard. The air freshener. The smoke. The lie that one conversation will stay only one conversation.

The INTERVENTION icon appears.

> A TRIGGER IS NOT AN INSTRUCTION.

This time, Mara does not have time to hate it.

The bell over the door jingles.

Her sister walks in.

Not because she knew.

Not because the app summoned her.

Because in this branch, the schedule changed three hours earlier. Her sister's ride fell through. The gas station was on the way. The room that was supposed to leave Mara alone fails to leave her alone.

That is the intervention.

Not a speech.

Presence.

Mara locks the phone facedown so fast it looks like guilt.

Her sister sees the motion.

She does not ask the wrong question.

She does not say, “Are you okay?”

That question has too many exits.

She says:

> Is it him?

The room changes.

The private loop becomes witnessed before it becomes action.

[[Mara]] feels anger first because witness can feel like interruption before it feels like care. Part of her wants to lie just to recover the privacy of the old path.

[[Jamal]] enters as form held open. Nothing has been sent yet. No record has hardened. The form is waiting, but it has not become evidence.

[[Maren]] enters as safety through proximity. The room steadies because Mara no longer has to be both player and witness.

[[Dev]] enters as access redirected. The door to Devin remains visible, but a different door opens beside it.

[[Teodor / Scott]] reads the branch as a design lesson: the strongest intervention is not always a better prompt. Sometimes the game has to create a way for another person to arrive before the player chooses.

The woman with the toddler still comes in.

This time, Mara's sister sees the math too.

Milk.

Banana.

Crackers.

Three dollars on pump four.

Mara reaches for her card, but her sister gets there first.

The register beeps.

The woman tries to object.

Both sisters say, “You're good,” at the same time.

The child gets the crackers.

Mara laughs once because the timing is stupid and human and exactly enough to break the private spell.

The phone vibrates under her palm.

The message is still unsent.

I miss you.

The words do not vanish.

They do not become false.

They become incomplete.

Mara deletes them while her sister watches.

Not as confession.

As shared room state.

Then she deletes the D.

Her sister does not clap.

No achievement unlocks.

She only says:

> Clock out when you can. I'll sit here until then.

The app opens by itself one last time.

PLAYER STATE: WITNESSED

DELAY RESPONSE: CONVERTED

RIPPLE CREATED

Mara looks at the empty parking lot and understands something that does not feel inspirational enough to be fake:

Sometimes the room changes because you become stronger.

Sometimes the room changes because you are no longer alone inside it.
