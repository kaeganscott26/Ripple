[[Chapter 03]] says the hallway finally becomes visible. Jamal names the room, Ms. Aranda adds context, the suspension line is crossed out, and the choke point changes.

The west stairwell still creates the incident.

The cart is still in the way.

## 📖 Alternate Story

Nobody blames the hallway.

Not at first.

Not later.

Not at all.

By the time Jamal reaches the office, the story has already beaten him there. Marcus is holding his shoulder. Devin has performed shock. Eli has watched enough to sound reliable. Ms. Aranda is still in her classroom because the door that opened in the canon path stayed closed in this one.

Principal Voss types with the clean confidence of a person entering an event after the room has already hidden itself.

Aggressive behavior.

Repeated pattern.

Failure to self-regulate.

Disruption.

Safety concern.

The form has plenty of boxes for Jamal.

It has no box for the cart.

No box for the trash cans.

No box for the west stairwell.

No box for the camera angle that could see the shove but not the setup.

No box for the sigh in algebra that entered Jamal's body as evidence before the hallway ever opened.

[[Jamal]] knows the truth is too large for the form, so he gives the smallest piece of it.

> He was in my way.

Principal Voss hears attitude.

The form hears confession.

The room hears nothing.

The suspension sticks.

Three days.

Three days at home while his mother works double shifts.

Three days for Marcus to become the story.

Three days for the west stairwell to keep eating moods.

Three days for the building to call itself neutral.

[[Mara]] enters this branch as absent witness. She knows what testimony can do, but no one has asked the right question in time.

[[Maren]] enters as mood collapse. The school feels calmer because Jamal has been removed, but the calm is false. The hallway is not safer. It is only less challenged.

[[Dev]] enters as blocked access. Jamal is told to walk away from a place the room never made walkable.

[[Teodor / Scott]] reads the branch as a design failure. The board recorded the output but failed to record the room state that produced it.

At home, Jamal does not feel free.

Suspension is supposed to be absence from school. In practice, it is school following him into a smaller room.

His mother gets the call during work.

She answers on the fourth try because her break finally comes. Her voice is already tired when Principal Voss explains what Jamal did. She asks what happened before that. Voss says they are still reviewing it. That means no.

When Jamal tries to explain, he hears himself sounding like every version of himself adults already expect.

The more he talks, the smaller the truth gets.

So he stops.

Silence becomes the safest lie.

On the second day, he opens his school portal and sees the missing assignments.

Zeros arrive before context.

On the third day, Marcus posts a joke about anger issues. Nobody says Jamal's name. They do not have to. The comments know where to stand.

By the re-entry meeting, the hallway has become irrelevant.

The question is not what room made the behavior make sense.

The question is how Jamal will improve.

He signs a behavior contract.

He agrees to use coping skills.

He agrees to walk away.

He agrees to request support.

He agrees to things that require paths the school has not built.

When he returns to the west stairwell, the cart is still there.

The trash cans are still there.

The trophy case still shines with old victories.

The hallway has eaten the consequence and left Jamal wearing the evidence.
