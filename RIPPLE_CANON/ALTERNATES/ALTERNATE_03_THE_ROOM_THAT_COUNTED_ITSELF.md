The opposite ripple is **complete accountability**.

---

## 📖 Alternate Story

The hallway does not disappear.

The schedule does not disappear.

The pressure, timing, lighting, tone, hunger, fatigue, memory, and authority in the room do not disappear.

In this path, when something goes wrong, the room does not point at one person first.

It pauses and asks what conditions helped produce the moment.

[[Jamal]] is not reduced to attitude, attendance, or tone. The room asks what paths were available to him and what paths only looked available from the outside.

[[Mara]] records what happened, but she does not confuse record with verdict.

[[Maren]] watches whether the room becomes safer after including itself or only more complicated.

[[Dev]] notices that access changes when the room admits its own architecture.

[[Teodor / Scott]] sees the first clear shape of [[THEORY_LAYER|Ripple]]: a person is never the only object in the event.

---

## 🟢 Intervention Point

Someone says:

> Before we decide who caused this, we have to count the room.

That sentence does not erase responsibility.

It makes responsibility more accurate.

---

## 🔴 Missed Intervention Point

The room rushes to assign blame because blame is faster than mapping conditions.

If missed, the person becomes the container for a whole architecture.

---

## 🌊 Ripple Event

Once the room counts itself, every future turn has a new requirement:

> No event can be fully interpreted until the room state is checked.
