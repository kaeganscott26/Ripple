# ALTERNATE 02 — THE TRIGGER THAT PASSED

_Mirrors: Chapter 02 — Trigger_

_Status: Ripple Canon | Alternate Path | Boundary Current_

## Core Mirror

Chapter 02 asks what happens when a trigger enters the room.

This alternate asks what happens when the trigger is recognized without being allowed to drive the whole turn.

The opposite ripple is pause.

## Alternate Story

Teodor / Scott feels the old pressure rise, but this time the room does not rush to explain it.

The pressure is real. The room does not deny it.

But the room also does not hand it the wheel.

A sentence appears like a handrail:

“This is a signal, not a command.”

Mara reads the pause as careful witness. She does not need the whole room to agree immediately. She only needs the next action to leave room for truth.

Jamal reads the pause as form refusing to harden too early. A feeling enters the room, but it does not become policy, accusation, or identity yet.

Maren reads the pause as safety. The question is not what the pressure proves. The question is whether the room can remain steady while it passes.

Dev reads the pause as a door kept open. The path remains available because pressure did not close it.

Teodor / Scott reads the pause as proof that a loop can be noticed before it finishes the turn.

## Intervention Point

Someone asks:

“What do you need before you decide what this means?”

The question does not argue with the pressure.

It slows the room down.

## Missed Intervention Point

The room treats the trigger as proof of character.

If missed, the person becomes the label instead of the room reading the event.

## Ripple Event

The pause becomes repeatable.

Other characters begin using it before major turns.

The board asks:

“Is this signal asking for care, or trying to become command?”

## Artifact Pulls

- Boundary Handrail can convert a missed turn into a pause.
- Mental Health Disclaimer stabilizes the room language.
- Masking Layer can hide pressure under competence.
- Boulder Protocol checks whether the next move comes from responsibility.
