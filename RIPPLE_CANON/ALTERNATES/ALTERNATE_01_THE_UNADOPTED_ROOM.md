# ALTERNATE 01 — THE UNADOPTED ROOM

_Mirrors: Chapter 01 — The First Ripple_

_Status: Ripple Canon | Alternate Path | Cultural / Clinical / Power Current_

## Core Mirror

Canonical Chapter 01 begins with the first ripple: a life enters one room and everything after it bends around that entry.

This alternate asks what happens if Teodor / Scott is never adopted into that room at all.

Not as punishment.

Not as fantasy.

As a board path.

The opposite ripple is absence before identity.

The room that shaped him never receives him. The family that would become his first frame never gets to name him son. The father who would buy the piano never becomes the father in that way. The mother who would become safety and wound never becomes the mother in that way. The first ripple does not strike the same floor.

So the board renders a life that begins without the original doorway.

## Alternate Story

In this path, the child remains inside systems before he has language for systems.

Files move before feelings do.

A name is written where a hand should have been.

No one is cruel in the first scene. That is the part the board makes difficult. The wound is not a villain entering the room. The wound is a room that never becomes permanent enough to feel like origin.

Teodor grows up learning that adults can be kind and temporary at the same time. He learns to read tone before rules. He learns which offices smell like paper, which waiting rooms have toys with missing pieces, and which voices mean he should be quiet because a decision is being made somewhere else.

His first theory is not Ripple Theory.

His first theory is: rooms decide before children understand what was decided.

Mara appears in this path as a case note before she becomes a witness. She is not the same person, but the board uses her token to represent the danger of testimony. Every adult writes something different about the child, and each version becomes partly true because another room believes it.

Jamal appears as the form itself: eligibility, placement, review, status. He is not blamed yet. He is bureaucracy before blame has a face.

Maren appears as the mood reader. She learns the child is safest when the room is predictable, but no one can promise predictability.

Dev appears as the visible door that does not open: a family considered, a home almost ready, a visit that goes well but does not become forever.

Teodor / Scott becomes The Unadopted Room: a child who learns that identity can be postponed by paperwork.

## Intervention Point

A stable adult tells the truth plainly:

“You are not a problem waiting for a room. The room is responsible for how it receives you.”

If this intervention lands, the child learns that movement is not the same as rejection.

## Missed Intervention Point

Every temporary room treats the child as adaptable because he is intelligent.

If missed, intelligence becomes camouflage. The child becomes good at surviving rooms without trusting them.

## Ripple Event

Because he is never adopted into the original family, the father layer changes.

The piano does not arrive.

Music arrives later, colder, through headphones instead of a living room.

AIFRED still exists, but in this path it begins as a tool for proving sound is real when people are not.

## Artifact Pulls

- Father Layer redirects this path toward grief for a father who never had the legal role.
- Form Layer increases institutional pressure.
- Masking Layer hides loneliness under intelligence.
- Boulder Protocol asks whether the child is being moved or carried.

## End States

### Intervention Ending

Teodor learns to build rooms without needing every room to keep him.

### Ripple Ending

He becomes a translator between people and systems because he knows what systems feel like from the inside.

### Missed Ending

He mistakes impermanence for proof that he should never fully arrive.

## Game Meaning

This alternate teaches the board that origin is not only where someone begins.

Origin is the first room that successfully tells the truth about receiving them.
