## 🧭 Core Mirror

[[Chapter 01]] says the first ripple reached [[Teodor / Scott]] through witness, broadcast, paperwork, movement, adoption, displacement, identity, longing, language, theory, book, and game.

This alternate asks what happens if the broadcast reaches the world but does **not** reach Teodor's future.

The camera still enters the hidden room.

The world still sees.

Some people still move.

But Teodor is not carried into the Oklahoma room.

The opposite ripple is **witness without rescue**.

The first circle enters the water, but it bends around him instead of lifting him.

---

## 📖 Alternate Story

Teodor does not know he is not leaving.

That is the first cruelty of this branch.

A child cannot miss the plane he never knew existed.

He cannot mourn the living room he never saw, the piano he never touched, the father who never bought the instrument, the son he never has, the kitchens he never survives, the code he never writes, or the game he never builds.

He only remains.

The room changes around him slowly.

After the broadcast, officials arrive with better words. The old language is cleaned. Rooms receive fresh paint, new records, foreign volunteers, donated clothes, forms, visiting doctors, cameras, translators, and people who promise that the nightmare has ended.

But systems do not end just because the world has looked at them.

A room can be witnessed and still remain a room.

Teodor grows inside the afterimage of rescue.

He is not invisible in the same way anymore. That almost makes it worse. He becomes visible enough to be counted, but not chosen enough to be carried. He becomes a name in a newer file, a child in a better photograph, a survivor of a system that now knows how to describe itself.

[[Mara]] enters this branch as the witness record. She is every camera, note, testimony, and outside eye that proves the room was real. Her path strengthens because the world cannot pretend it never saw.

[[Jamal]] enters as the file that keeps moving. He is status, eligibility, institutional language, late paperwork, closed windows, delayed signatures, and the official sentence that says not this child, not this time.

[[Maren]] enters as the room's mood. She notices that improvement can still feel like abandonment when it arrives without belonging.

[[Dev]] enters as the door that almost opens. There is a family who asks. There is a placement considered. There is a path visible on paper. But visible access is not access.

[[Teodor / Scott]] remains Teodor.

He keeps the language of his birth, but loses the alternate language that would have built Ripple.

He keeps the streets, but loses the Oklahoma room.

He keeps continuity, but loses the rupture that would have become theory.

He becomes a man who understands systems from below but never receives the toolchain that would turn that understanding into a playable archive.

There is no [[AIFRED]] in this branch.

There is no version of Ripple called Ripple.

There is no game in the form the player is holding now.

But the pattern does not disappear.

A ripple can fail to rescue one life and still move through another.

In this branch, Mara carries the first version of the game as a witness archive. She does not build it as software. She builds it as record. Names, dates, rooms, testimonies, missed interventions, maps of systems that claimed they had already been fixed.

Jamal carries the second version as form pressure. The game becomes policy language before it becomes play: a model for asking which rooms produced which outcomes.

Maren carries the third version as mood memory. She knows that the room is not safe just because it looks improved.

Dev carries the fourth version as access logic. He tracks doors that were visible but never available.

Teodor does not create Ripple.

Teodor becomes the reason someone else has to.
