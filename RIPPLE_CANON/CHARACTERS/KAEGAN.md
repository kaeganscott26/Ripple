## 🧭 Core Role

Kaegan is not a reward state.

Kaegan is not proof that [[Teodor / Scott]] healed.

Kaegan is not the father’s intervention point.

Kaegan is **Player 0826**: a conditional character who unlocks only when the Teodor/Scott lineage path allows him to exist and when the game preserves his agency.

He enters the game as a player, not as a cure.

---

## 🔐 Existence Rule

Kaegan’s character depends on Teodor/Scott’s origin branch.

### ✅ Canon Origin

If Teodor/Scott is adopted through the canon path, Kaegan exists and can unlock as Player 0826.

### ⚠️ Late Adoption Origin

If [[ALTERNATE_01B_THE_BROADCAST_THAT_NEVER_REACHED]] is active, Kaegan can exist only if the later adoption / Builder Path remains open.

### ❌ Unadopted Origin

If [[ALTERNATE_01_THE_UNADOPTED_ROOM]] is active and Teodor was never adopted, Kaegan becomes obsolete for that run because the lineage path does not exist.

In that branch, Kaegan is not dead, missing, or erased inside the story.

He simply never becomes part of that reality.

The ripple must migrate to another carrier.

---

## 🧬 Three Reality States

### 🟢 Reality 01 — Canon Kaegan / Small Signal

Source: [[Chapter 08]]

Scott sends a small birthday message. Kaegan replies with a small bridge. The father practices not grabbing.

Core mechanic:

> Small signal. Agency preserved. Love without possession.

### 🔴 Reality 02 — Boundary Kaegan / Love As Load

Source: [[ALTERNATE_08_THE_DATE_THAT_ANSWERED]]

Scott sends love with too much emotional weight. Kaegan receives the love but also feels the load. Silence becomes boundary data.

Core mechanic:

> A message can be full of love and still be too heavy to receive.

### 🌊 Reality 03 — Player Kaegan / Safe Invitation

Source: [[ALTERNATE_08B_THE_GAME_THAT_REACHED_HIM]]

Scott sends a playable gift without surveillance or demand. Kaegan can play, laugh, ignore, close, or return on his own time.

Core mechanic:

> Kaegan enters the game only as a player, never as the father’s cure.

## 🎮 Move Logic

If Kaegan is active and a father/child move appears, the board must check:

1. Is Kaegan being treated as a person or a proof state?
2. Is the message or gift small enough to receive?
3. Can Kaegan refuse, delay, ignore, or close the door without punishment?
4. Is Scott seeking connection or evidence?
5. Does the move preserve Kaegan’s observer reality?

If the answer fails, route to Boundary State.

If the answer succeeds, allow Player 0826 progress.

---

## 🧠 Design Rule

Kaegan makes the game more playable because he gives the player a real reason to care about the birthday route.

But the game must protect him from becoming the object it is trying to critique.

He can be funny.

He can be skeptical.

He can roast the game.

He can walk away.

He can come back later.

He can play without fixing the father.

That is the point.

---

## 🎮 Game Meaning

Kaegan is the proof that the father/child layer only works if the child remains free.

The board can invite him.

It cannot claim him.
