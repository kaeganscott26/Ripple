# 🌀 Community Ripple Guide

INTERVENTION is complete as a book, but the room does not end when the reader closes the repo.

If the story makes you notice something in your own life, you are invited to document it carefully.

Not as proof.

Not as confession bait.

Not as a performance.

As a field note.

---

## 🗝️ Canon Boundary

The seventeen chapters are complete.

The official artifact layer is curated.

Community submissions are not automatically canon.

Readers can add echoes.

Only the curator decides what becomes part of the room.

A pull request is not permission to rewrite INTERVENTION.

A pull request is a suggestion placed at the doorway.

---

## 🗝️ Ways to Participate

You can participate in three ways:

1. **Open an Issue** for a short ripple log or intervention point.
2. **Start a Discussion** for longer reflections, questions, or reader responses.
3. **Submit a Pull Request** only for typo fixes, documentation improvements, or curated community echoes.

The goal is not to make the biggest post.

The goal is to notice the room without damaging it.

---

## 🧭 What to Share

Good submissions usually describe one small moment clearly.

Examples:

- A sentence changed the mood of a room.
- A pause interrupted an old loop.
- A place made a behavior easier or harder.
- A song, post, or message became a signal somewhere else.
- A child, parent, friend, stranger, coworker, or memory became an intervention point.
- A door existed before you could perceive it as available.
- A ripple you created came back as feedback.

Small is better than dramatic.

Ordinary is allowed.

The room is not always dramatic when it changes.

---

## ⚠️ Rules of the Room

No private information.

No full names of people who did not consent.

No addresses, phone numbers, workplaces, screenshots with identifying details, or private messages unless everything identifying has been removed.

No diagnosing strangers.

No targeting real people.

No harassment.

No conspiracy escalation.

No crisis posting as gameplay.

No asking the repo, the readers, or the author to replace real-world support.

No pull requests that rewrite chapters, alter canon, or add real people into the official story without explicit approval.

No story outranks the body.

No metaphor outranks safety.

No framework outranks consent.

No ripple excuses harm.

If you are unsafe, contact a trusted real person, local emergency support, or a crisis line in the world you physically inhabit.

## 📝 Issue Template: Ripple Event

Use this shape when opening an Issue:

```text
ROOM:
Where did this happen?

RIPPLE:
What moved through the room?

INTERVENTION POINT:
What interrupted the pattern, if anything?

FEEDBACK:
What changed afterward?

BOUNDARY CHECK:
Did you remove private details and protect real people?
```

---

## 📝 Issue Template: Missed Intervention

Use this shape when the ripple is about something that did not happen:

```text
ROOM:
Where was the missed moment?

WHAT COULD HAVE HAPPENED:
What intervention was possible?

WHAT HAPPENED INSTEAD:
What did the system do automatically?

WHAT YOU SEE NOW:
What changed because you noticed it later?

BOUNDARY CHECK:
Did you remove private details and protect real people?
```

---

## 🗃️ Community Echo Submissions

Community echoes should live in `COMMUNITY/`, not inside the official chapter sequence.

They should feel found, not forced.

Keep them short.

Keep them safe.

Keep them connected to the architecture of INTERVENTION.

Suggested format:

```text
ECHO: [short title]
ROOM: [where the ripple appeared]
LAYER: [body / room / group / family / work / signal / loop / door / network / cosmos / grief / inheritance]
STATUS: [noticed / traveling / interrupted / unresolved]

FIELD NOTE:
[short fragment, reflection, log, or recovered text]
```

---

## 🔚 Exit

The repo is not here to make you believe fiction is real.

It is here to ask what you do after fiction makes you notice reality differently.

Participate gently.

Leave the room safer than you found it.

Fine.
