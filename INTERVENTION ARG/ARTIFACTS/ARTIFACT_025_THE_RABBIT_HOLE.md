# ARTIFACT_025_THE_RABBIT_HOLE

_Status: Posted Fragment | Addiction Current | Split-Self Symbol | Reality Remains Reality_

```text
Hello my name is Scott.

Let me introduce you to my pet rabbit.

He is a wonderful companion, but he has only one flaw.

He digs holes.

He cannot help it.

It is an impulse.

An addiction.

A way out.

Something he can crawl into and hide inside.

Something that keeps him from showing his face to the ugly truth that is the world.

His name is Teodor.
```

---

## 🐇 Field Note

This artifact appeared as a short public fragment after the repo entered its final polish state.

It reads like a joke at first.

Then the metaphor turns.

The rabbit is not only a rabbit.

The hole is not only a hole.

The digging is not only an animal behavior.

The fragment uses the image of a pet rabbit to describe the part of a person that escapes downward when the world becomes too much.

It connects addiction, shame, avoidance, survival behavior, dissociation, hiding, and split identity without explaining all of them directly.

The final line changes the room:

> **His name is Teodor.**

That line connects the artifact to [[Chapter 17]], where Teodor becomes part of the return current: adoption, naming, inheritance, grief, fatherhood, accountability, and the buried self.

---

## 🧭 Ripple Reading

```text
pain / pressure
  ↓
impulse to escape
  ↓
hole becomes refuge
  ↓
refuge becomes pattern
  ↓
pattern becomes identity
  ↓
identity hides from the room
  ↓
name reveals the split
```

The rabbit does not dig because he is evil.

He digs because digging worked once.

He digs because the hole offered relief.

He digs because the dark felt safer than being seen.

He digs because down was still a direction.

That is the addiction current.

Not moral failure.

Not excuse.

A loop.

A behavior that once protected the self can later trap the self.

## ⚠️ Guardrail First

This artifact is metaphor.

It is not a diagnosis.

It is not a clinical claim by itself.

It is not permission to romanticize addiction, relapse, avoidance, or self-destruction.

Addiction should be handled through grounded language, real support, treatment when needed, community, accountability, and safety.

No metaphor outranks recovery.

No rabbit hole outranks the waking room.

No hidden self outranks the person who still has to live.

Reality remains reality.

## 🧠 Clinical Current

The rabbit hole connects strongly to [[CLINICAL_REALITY_LAYER]].

Avoidance can become a coping strategy.

Coping strategies can become habits.

Habits can become loops.

Loops can become identity if they are repeated long enough.

That does not mean the person is the loop.

It means the loop has been practiced.

Ripple Theory reads this as:

```text
hurt
  ↓
relief behavior
  ↓
reinforcement
  ↓
repetition
  ↓
automatic response
  ↓
identity confusion
  ↓
intervention point
```

The intervention point is not shame.

The intervention point is noticing the hole before climbing into it.

---

## 🕳️ The Hole

A hole can be shelter.

A hole can be hiding.

A hole can be a relapse pattern.

A hole can be silence.

A hole can be a screen.

A hole can be a drug.

A hole can be sleep.

A hole can be fantasy.

A hole can be rage.

A hole can be the old room pretending to be safety.

The question is not only:

> Why does he dig?

The question is:

> What did the first hole save him from?

And then:

> What is the hole costing him now?
