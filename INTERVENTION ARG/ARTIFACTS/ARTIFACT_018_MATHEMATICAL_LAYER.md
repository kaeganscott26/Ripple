## The Loop

Take a number.

Double it.

If the result has more than one digit, reduce it by adding the digits together until one digit remains.

Starting at `1`, the reduced doubling loop is:

```text
1 → 2 → 4 → 8 → 7 → 5 → 1
```

Expanded:

```text
1 doubled is 2
2 doubled is 4
4 doubled is 8
8 doubled is 16 → 1 + 6 = 7
7 doubled is 14 → 1 + 4 = 5
5 doubled is 10 → 1 + 0 = 1
```

Then the loop begins again.

This is a real arithmetic pattern.

In ordinary math language, this is digital-root behavior connected to arithmetic modulo 9.

The visible loop is not a door by itself.

The loop is a mirror.

## What Is Missing

The reduced doubling path from `1` does not pass through:

```text
3, 6, 9
```

Those numbers form different reduced doubling behavior:

```text
3 → 6 → 3
9 → 9
```

Expanded:

```text
3 doubled is 6
6 doubled is 12 → 1 + 2 = 3

9 doubled is 18 → 1 + 8 = 9
```

That is the real mathematical layer.

Anything beyond that is interpretation.
