## 🪨 Field Note

Boulder Protocol:

1. Name the room.
2. Check base reality.
3. Check who may be affected.
4. Check why you want to act.
5. Choose the smallest useful action.
6. Accept that the water will move without your permission.
7. Do not chase every wave.
8. Come back with care if damage occurs.
9. Let the ripple belong to the room after release.
10. Leave a door, not a cage.

Final prompt:

> What are you carrying?

## 🧭 Ripple Theory Connection

The Boulder Protocol is the action guardrail for Ripple Theory.

A ripple can be intentional without being controllable.

Once a signal enters the room, the room becomes part of the signal.

```text
intention
      ↓
action
      ↓
room response
      ↓
secondary ripple
      ↓
unowned consequence
      ↓
return with care
```

The protocol does not prevent consequence.

It teaches responsibility before release.
