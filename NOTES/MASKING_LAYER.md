## 🎚️ Core Signal
Masking is what happens when one signal becomes loud enough to hide another signal.

In audio, masking happens when one frequency covers another frequency so completely that the listener no longer hears what is underneath it.

In human reality, masking happens when one narrative becomes loud enough to cover another truth.

The hidden truth does not disappear.

It becomes inaudible.

## 🧠 Plain Language Meaning
The Masking Layer describes the stories humans tell themselves so they can get through each day.

Sometimes those stories protect us.

Sometimes they trap us.

Sometimes they begin as survival.

Sometimes they become lies.

A lie can become part of reality when enough memory gathers around it.

Not because the lie becomes objectively true.

Because people start acting as if it is true.

That action changes the room.

The changed room changes memory.

The changed memory changes what others perceive.

Eventually, people inherit the masked version of the event instead of the original signal.

## 🪞 Narrative Mask
A narrative mask is the version of reality a person wears so they can keep functioning.

Examples:

- “It did not hurt me.”
- “I had no choice.”
- “That was just how things were.”
- “They meant well.”
- “I am fine.”
- “It was not that bad.”
- “This is normal.”
- “This is who I am.”

These statements may not begin as lies.

They may begin as emotional scaffolding.

But if the mask is repeated long enough, the room may begin treating the mask as the person.

## 🌊 Ripple Function
The Masking Layer explains how perceived reality can overpower base reality.

Base reality asks:

What happened?

Perceived reality asks:

What did the person believe happened?

Social reality asks:

What did the room agree happened?

Institutional reality asks:

What version was recorded, enforced, or inherited?

Masking happens when the loudest version survives, even if it is not the clearest version.

## 🔊 AIFRED Connection
AIFRED understood masking first as an audio problem.

A loud frequency can cover a quieter one.

A dominant tone can hide detail.

A compressed signal can feel powerful while destroying movement.

Ripple inherits this idea and applies it to rooms, memory, and society.

In Ripple terms:

**Memory Masking** is when a dominant interpretation covers a quieter truth until no one believes the quieter truth was ever audible.

## 🪬 Philosophical Note
Humans do not only lie to deceive.

Sometimes humans lie to survive.

Sometimes humans believe the lie because belief is the only thing holding the room together.

But once a lie becomes memory, it does not stay private.

It changes how others perceive the person wearing it.

It changes what the room expects.

It changes what the next person inherits.

That is how masking manifests reality.

Not by changing the original event.

By changing what can still be heard after the event.

## 🌀 Closing Line
The loudest story is not always the truest story.

Sometimes it is only the one that survived the mix.
