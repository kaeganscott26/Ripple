## 1. Known model

These are ideas drawn from accepted or commonly used scientific framing.

They are included as grounding language, not as original claims.

### Cosmology

- The universe expands.
- In standard cosmology, expansion is described with the scale factor `a(t)`.
- The expansion rate can be described by the Hubble parameter `H(t)`.
- Current cosmological models include matter, radiation, curvature, and dark energy.
- Dark energy is often modeled as a cosmological constant with equation-of-state parameter `w = -1`.
- Observations such as the cosmic microwave background, baryon acoustic oscillations, supernova distances, galaxy clustering, and structure growth are used to test expansion models.

### Relativity and time

- Relativity changes the simple idea of one universal clock.
- Time and space are treated together as spacetime.
- Observers can disagree about timing and distance depending on motion and gravity.
- Near singularities or early-universe limits, current models reach places where deeper physics may be needed.

### Human systems

- People do not experience reality as raw data.
- Perception, memory, emotion, language, environment, and social feedback shape how events are interpreted.
- Small actions can change group behavior, emotional temperature, and future choices.

### Shape and repeated geometry

- Similar shapes can appear across scale when similar constraints appear.
- Spirals, waves, branches, vortices, and toroidal patterns can emerge from different systems for different reasons.
- Similar shape is a clue, not proof of identical mechanism.

## 2. Speculative bridge

These are Ripple Theory proposals.

They are not presented as proven science.

They are questions and models that attempt to connect human-scale consequence with cosmic-scale emergence.

### Core speculation

> **Existence is the first ripple.**

If existence is the first ripple we can observe from inside reality, then the deeper question becomes:

> **What is existence a consequence of?**

Ripple Theory does not begin by naming the source.

It begins by asking whether the ripple can be noticed, modeled, or measured.

### Cosmic bridge

Ripple Theory proposes a possible scale-stack:

```text
UNKNOWN / DEEPER LAYER
        ↓
FIRST RIPPLE: EXISTENCE
        ↓
SPACETIME / EXPANSION / LIGHT
        ↓
MATTER / GRAVITY / STRUCTURE
        ↓
LIFE / ADAPTATION / MEMORY
        ↓
CONSCIOUSNESS / PERCEPTION / LANGUAGE
        ↓
CHOICE / CONSEQUENCE / INTERVENTION
        ↓
STORY / MEANING / ARG / ECHO
```

This is not a proof chain.

It is a framework for asking how consequence may scale.

### Geometry bridge

Ripple Theory also asks whether consequence leaves shape behind.

> **Geometry is consequence made visible.**

At the human scale, consequence may appear as mood, behavior, repair, avoidance, or change.

At the story scale, consequence may appear as chapters, artifacts, reader echoes, and repository links.

At the cosmic scale, consequence may appear as expansion, curvature, structure, clustering, or repeated geometry.

Toroidal circulation is one possible visual metaphor for feedback-based systems:

```text
source → outward flow → curved return → re-entry → next ripple
```

This does not mean the universe is proven to be a torus.

It means toroidal structure is a useful bridge for thinking about expansion, feedback, and return.

### Mathematical placeholder

A speculative deeper causal or time-like field can be represented as:

```text
phi(t)
```

If such a field affects cosmic expansion, its energy density could be represented as:

```text
rho_phi(t)
```

A first-pass expansion shape would be:

```text
H(t)^2 = (8 * pi * G / 3) * (rho_m + rho_r + rho_phi(t))
```

The important point is not that this equation proves Ripple Theory.

The important point is that it gives the speculation a place where measurement could challenge it.

### Testable hook

If dark energy is constant:

```text
w = -1
wa = 0
```

If dark energy is dynamic:

```text
w(z) != -1
```

or:

```text
w(z) = w0 + wa * z / (1 + z)
```

A dynamic-ripple model becomes weaker if better future observations keep supporting a perfect constant.

> **The first ripple becomes science only when it predicts something the universe can say no to.**

## 4. Participation prompts

Use these questions carefully.

They are prompts for reflection, not instructions to treat coincidence as command.

### Human scale

> After reading this, notice a possible fork in your day. Did you choose, did you hesitate, did you see the pattern, or did you miss the moment?

### Story scale

> What changed in your perception after entering the room? Did the chapter alter the way you interpreted a real event?

### Cosmic scale

> What would the universe need to do for you to change your model?

### Geometry scale

> What shape did the consequence leave behind?

### Cultural scale

> What older human framework does this resemble, and does that resemblance support the theory, challenge it, or expose a weakness?

### Unexplained scale

> What experience exceeded your current map, what category did you give it, and did that interpretation ground you or destabilize you?

### Safety scale

> Is this noticing helping you become more grounded, responsible, and connected — or is it making reality feel less stable?

If the answer is less stable, step back.

No theory outranks safety.
