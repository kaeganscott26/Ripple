# TEODOR / SCOTT BOARD MAP

_Status: Character Board Map | 72-Space Life Board | Fixed Anchors | Branch Pair Skeleton | RIPPLE Game Engine Source_

---

## Purpose

This document defines the first full board map for the Teodor / Scott playable character path in **Ripple: The Living Board**.

This board is built from the Teodor / Scott base story.

The player is not moving through a generic board.

The player is moving through one possible version of a life.

Each space represents a fixed event, emotional fork, memory fragment, missed possibility, or consequence point.

The final story generated at the Last Glass should not invent a random ending. It should create one possible fiction from the base story, changed by what the player collected, ignored, missed, or was forced to receive.

---

## Core Rule

The board does not ask:

> What random story should happen?

The board asks:

> Which version of Teodor / Scott did this run create?

---

## Board Length

The Teodor / Scott board contains **72 spaces**.

This gives the player enough movement, missed spaces, forks, and collected fragments to create a full fictional ending with real cause and effect.

---

# Board Zones

## Zone 1 — Origin

**Spaces 01–08**

This zone begins with adoption and naming.

It establishes Teodor as the first name, Scott as the given name, and identity as something layered rather than replaced.

The player does not decide whether adoption happened.

The player decides how the character carries it.

---

## Zone 2 — Childhood Rooms

**Spaces 09–16**

This zone covers early home life, warmth, loneliness, ordinary rooms, watching from behind his own eyes, and the first sense that belonging can be real and complicated at the same time.

Childhood should be allowed to hold contradiction.

The board should never flatten this section into only happy or only sad unless the run creates that version.

---

## Zone 3 — Music and Fear

**Spaces 17–24**

This zone covers the haunted room, listening, fear, pattern recognition, imagination, piano, and music as a survival space.

Fear does not only scare the character.

Fear teaches him to pay attention.

Music does not only comfort him.

Music gives structure to what cannot be explained.

---

## Zone 4 — Father Layer

**Spaces 25–32**

This zone covers father as presence, father as anchor, father as absence, the empty chair, grief, and the way one death tilts the board.

The father must feel human here.

Not symbolic first.

Human first.

Funny. Stubborn. Practical. Flawed. Present. Missed.

---

## Zone 5 — Collapse and Survival

**Spaces 33–40**

This zone covers rebellion, survival, damage, stubbornness, bad choices, consequences, and attempts to repair.

The board must not flatter the character.

Some wounds explain behavior.

They do not excuse all behavior.

---

## Zone 6 — Kaegan / Future Layer

**Spaces 41–48**

This zone covers fatherhood, love, distance, future responsibility, missed calls, repair, and the realization that a son changes what consequences mean.

Kaegan is not a side path.

Kaegan is the future space.

After Kaegan, Scott’s choices have another address.

---

## Zone 7 — Work / Systems Layer

**Spaces 49–56**

This zone covers kitchens, timing, pressure, tickets, stations, work rhythm, emotional fields, and practical systems learned before they were called theory.

The kitchen becomes a live model of Ripple Theory.

A room changes when one person changes their rhythm.

---

## Zone 8 — AIFRED / INTERVENTION

**Spaces 57–64**

This zone covers AIFRED, audio comparison, diagnostic mirrors, current state versus target state, INTERVENTION as archive, and the act of arranging pain into structure.

AIFRED begins as an audio tool.

INTERVENTION becomes the filing cabinet inside the computer.

---

## Zone 9 — Ripple / Last Glass

**Spaces 65–72**

This zone covers the naming of the pattern, accountability, wounds turned into monuments, repair, failure, beginning again, and the Last Glass.

Ripple is not victory.

Ripple is responsibility.

The final space is the Last Glass.

# Fixed Anchors

Fixed anchors are spaces that must remain true in the Teodor / Scott board.

The player may collect, ignore, miss, or be forced into the meaning of these spaces.

But the final story cannot pretend these events do not exist.

# Branch Pair Rule

Branch pairs create the alternate-life logic of the board.

If the player collects one side of a branch pair, that version becomes dominant.

If the player collects both, the final story carries contradiction.

If the player ignores one, that refusal becomes pressure.

If the player misses both, the board resolves that branch as ambiguous, random, blended, or hidden depending on mode.

---

# Mode Behavior for Missed Branches

## Mystery Mode

The board resolves missed branches silently.

The player only discovers the effect in the final story.

## Vague Mode

The board hints that something unresolved affected the final story.

## Experimental / Master Mode

The run summary shows which branch group was missed and how the board resolved it.
