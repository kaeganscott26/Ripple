# RIPPLE RULES

_Status: Simulation Rules | Consequence Engine | Reality Layers_

A ripple is any input that enters the board and changes interpretation.

A ripple may be physical, social, emotional, symbolic, or informational.

---

## Core Rule

A ripple does not need to be true to become consequential.

Truth and consequence are related, but not identical.

---

## Ripple Types

### Observation Ripple

The player inspects something.

The inspected object becomes more important.

### Naming Ripple

The player or agent names an object, room, pattern, or fear.

Naming may increase institutional weight.

### Artifact Ripple

An artifact is moved, discovered, hidden, named, or misremembered.

### Rumor Ripple

A claim spreads between agents.

The claim may mutate as it moves.

### Law Ripple

A rule is proposed or passed.

The rule may stabilize or cage the board.

### Memory Ripple

A memory is revealed, suppressed, distorted, or inherited.

### Absence Ripple

Something missing becomes meaningful.

Absence can organize behavior.

### Observer Ripple

The player injects text, moves attention, or changes conditions.

Agents may eventually develop observer suspicion.

---

## Reality Layer Transform

Every major ripple should update four layers.

### Base Reality

What happened.

### Perceived Reality

What each agent thinks happened.

### Social Reality

What the group repeats or accepts.

### Institutional Reality

What hardens into rule, ritual, archive, role, taboo, or law.

---

## Mutation Rule

Ripples should mutate through agents.

An agent repeats a ripple through their own:

- memory seed
- fear
- desire
- relationship map
- trust map
- role
- current pressure

The same claim should not always spread the same way.

---

## Institution Rule

A belief becomes institutional when it creates structure.

Examples:

- witness law
- night watch
- archive category
- taboo
- ritual
- access rule
- faction boundary
- origin doctrine

Institutions do not have to begin from true beliefs.

They begin from consequential beliefs.

---

## Nested Inheritance Rule

If agents create an inner simulation, they pass down only what they can observe or encode.

Source truth may become distorted through inheritance.

Artifact becomes story.

Story becomes myth.

Myth becomes law.

Law becomes physics.

---

## Safety Rule

Ripple is a game system.

It should never tell players that real life is fake or that the game is proof of a hidden reality.

Keep the framing grounded:

Ripple models how observers inside a limited system generate meaning from available signals.
